This is an invitation to join a virtual exam prep team for Chemistry 130. The team will follow an organized online process to filter through 400+ problems relevant to Exam 1 in Problem Roulette database and bubble up the 25 problems you collectively find most relevant to the first exam.

Benefits to you personally?:

  1. It will help you reflect on what you're studying and what others are studying :)
  2. You will see anonymously where you stand compared to your classmates on all the problems you do!
  3. It will help you make studying slightly more fun :)
Participation Requirements (~3 min + ~1 hour + ~10 min + ~5 min + ~1.5 hour <= 3.0 hours):
  1. ~3 minutes -- Make sure you understand the first two steps
    • Watch this 3 minute video SHOWING you how to do the first two steps
    • --Do NOW, before you get started--
  2. ~1 hour -- Take a simulated online exam:
    • Follow this special link to problem roulette and do as many problems as you can in 1 hour. The link will automatically select the right set of problems
    • Everyone will receive completely random draws from the full set of over 400+ previous exam problems in the Problem Roulette database
    • Treat this hour of practice like the real exam for your own benefit !!!
    • Notice: We will add a few more problems to PR mid week, so those students who complete this step after that will draw from a few more problems.
    • --Due by Sunday Oct 6th at midnight--
  3. ~10 minutes -- Select a "best" problem:
    • Review your PR history under the 'My Summary' tab and select one problem which "you think captures something everyone should understand well" for the upcoming exam
    • Copy the link identifier to the problem's google doc located at the very bottom of the summary page
    • Submit the link identifier as your problem choice (aka game) on the tournament registration page
    • --Due by Sunday Oct 6th at midnight--
  4. ~5 minutes -- Compare two problems & eliminate one:
    • Visit the voting page of our tournament application and decide between the two problems presented to you.
    • Select the one "you think is going to be more important to understand in order to do well on the upcoming exam"
    • --Due by Friday Oct 11th. Available beginning Oct 7th--
  5. ~1.5 hours -- Take the "bubbled up practice exam":
    • Participants will use this 2nd special link in PR you will be able to take the "crowd sourced" practice exam
    • Everyone will get the same problems on this practice exam, and it will be timed
    • On each problem of the practice exam compare your answer and time spent with the anonymous data about your classmates
    • --Available beginning Oct 11th--

Enjoy :)
